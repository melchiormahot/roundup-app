## Tax Framework Summary

| Country | Standard Deduction | Enhanced Rate | Cap |
|---------|-------------------|---------------|-----|
| **France** | 66% tax reduction | 75% (Loi Coluche: charities helping people in difficulty with food, shelter, healthcare) | 66% up to 20% of taxable income; 75% up to EUR 2,000 (since Oct 2025) |
| **UK** | Gift Aid: charity reclaims 20% basic rate | Higher/additional rate taxpayers claim back difference (20% or 25% extra relief) | No cap on Gift Aid |
| **Germany** | Deduction from taxable income (marginal rate applies) | Foundations: up to EUR 1M per decade | 20% of annual income |
| **Spain** | 80% on first EUR 250 | 35% above EUR 250 (40% if 3+ consecutive years to same charity) | 10% of taxable income |
| **Belgium** | 30% tax reduction (reduced from 45% as of 1 Jan 2025) | None | 10% of net income or EUR 392,200 |

## Notes

- **"% to Programs" figures** marked (est.) are informed estimates based on sector averages and available data. Where exact figures were found in annual reports, those are used directly.
- **Loi Coluche (France):** The 75% enhanced rate applies only to charities providing direct aid to people in difficulty (food, shelter, medical care). Most health, environment, education, animal, and human rights charities qualify only for the standard 66% rate.
- **Belgium 2025 change:** The Belgian coalition government reduced the charitable donation tax reduction from 45% to 30% effective 1 January 2025.
- **Germany:** Tax deduction works differently from France/Belgium; donations reduce taxable income rather than providing a direct tax credit. The effective tax benefit depends on the donor's marginal tax rate (14% to 45%).
- **Spain:** The 80% rate on the first EUR 250 makes small donations extremely tax-efficient. The loyalty bonus (40% instead of 35% for 3+ years of consecutive giving to the same charity) incentivises recurring donations.
